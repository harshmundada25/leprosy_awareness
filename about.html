Understanding Leprosy & Anandwan’s Role

Leprosy is a curable disease, yet myths and stigma remain. Anandwan has transformed lives through rehabilitation.

What is Leprosy?

Leprosy, also known as Hansen’s disease, is a chronic infectious disease caused by Mycobacterium leprae. It primarily affects the skin, peripheral nerves, mucous membranes, and eyes. The disease progresses slowly, and early detection is crucial to prevent severe complications.

Fact: Leprosy has been known for over 4,000 years and was historically feared due to a lack of understanding. Today, it is fully curable with multi-drug therapy (MDT).

Anandwan: A Haven for Leprosy Patients

Founded by Baba Amte in 1948, Anandwan is a self-sustaining community in Warora, Maharashtra. It provides rehabilitation, medical care, and vocational training to people affected by leprosy and other disabilities.

Anandwan promotes dignity, self-reliance, and empowerment by transforming lives through education, skill development, and community support.

How Does Leprosy Spread?

Leprosy spreads through prolonged close contact with an untreated person, mainly via respiratory droplets. However, it is not highly contagious. Most people have natural immunity, and early treatment prevents further transmission.

Symptoms of Leprosy

Symptoms appear gradually and can take years to develop. Common signs include:

  • Discolored patches of skin with loss of sensation.
  • Numbness or tingling in the hands and feet.
  • Muscle weakness, paralysis, or deformities in advanced cases.
  • Eye problems that can lead to blindness.

Without treatment, leprosy can cause permanent nerve damage and disabilities.

Leprosy in Numbers

According to the World Health Organization (WHO):

  • Over 200,000 new cases of leprosy are detected worldwide every year.
  • More than 3 million people live with disabilities caused by leprosy.
  • India, Brazil, and Indonesia account for nearly 80% of new cases.

Leprosy still exists, but with awareness and timely treatment, it can be eliminated.

Treatment and Cure

Leprosy is completely curable with multi-drug therapy (MDT), which is provided free of cost by the WHO.

MDT consists of a combination of Rifampicin, Clofazimine, and Dapsone, taken for 6 to 12 months depending on the severity.

Early diagnosis and treatment can prevent disabilities and ensure a normal life.

Breaking the Stigma

Despite being curable, leprosy is still associated with discrimination and social exclusion. Many patients suffer from psychological distress due to stigma.

Anandwan is a living example of how acceptance and support can help leprosy patients lead fulfilling lives.

Raising awareness and spreading knowledge helps eliminate fear and myths surrounding the disease.
