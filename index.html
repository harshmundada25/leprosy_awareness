What is Leprosy?

Leprosy, or Hansenâ€™s disease, is a chronic infectious disease caused by Mycobacterium leprae. It affects the skin, peripheral nerves, and eyes, and if untreated, can cause disabilities.

Leprosy is not highly contagious and spreads through prolonged close contact. Awareness and early treatment prevent complications.

Symptoms & Treatment

Symptoms develop gradually and include:

  • Pale or reddish skin patches with reduced sensation.
  • Numbness or tingling in hands and feet.
  • Muscle weakness and deformities in advanced cases.

Treatment: Leprosy is curable with multi-drug therapy (MDT), provided free of cost by WHO.

Anandwan - A Community of Courage

Anandwan, located in Warora, Chandrapur, was founded in 1949 by social activist Baba Amte to rehabilitate those affected by leprosy. Today, it is a self-sustained community that provides healthcare, education, and employment opportunities.

With vocational training and social acceptance, Anandwan proves that those affected by leprosy can lead dignified and independent lives.

Why Awareness Matters?

Myths and stigma prevent timely treatment. Raising awareness leads to early detection, reducing disabilities, and eliminating misconceptions about leprosy.
