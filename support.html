Support & Resources

Find the help you need for treatment, financial aid, and rehabilitation.

1. NGOs & Organizations

Several organizations provide free treatment, awareness programs, and rehabilitation for leprosy patients.

  • WHO Leprosy Program: Free Multi-Drug Therapy (MDT) for all patients worldwide.
  • The Leprosy Mission (TLM): Offers medical aid, rehabilitation, and social support.
  • American Leprosy Missions: Works in over 40 countries to provide free treatment.
  • ILEP (International Federation of Anti-Leprosy Associations): Supports research and patient care.
  • Sasakawa Health Foundation: Focuses on ending discrimination against leprosy patients.
  • Anandwan: A community founded by Baba Amte, offering rehabilitation, education, and employment for leprosy-affected individuals and their families.

2. Government & Private Support

Many governments have programs to assist leprosy patients with treatment and rehabilitation.

  • National Leprosy Eradication Program (NLEP): Free diagnosis and treatment services.
  • Ayushman Bharat: Provides free healthcare and insurance for affected families.
  • Social Welfare Schemes: Monthly financial aid for patients under disability programs.
  • Public Health Centers (PHCs): Free checkups and treatment in local hospitals.

3. Financial Aid & Rehabilitation

Financial aid and skill training programs help leprosy patients regain independence.

  • Livelihood Restoration Programs: Help patients start small businesses.
  • Leprosy Colony Grants: Funds for housing and rehabilitation centers.
  • Skill Development Training: Free vocational training for employment.
  • Scholarships for Children: Education support for children of leprosy-affected families.
